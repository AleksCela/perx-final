The opportunity
Across Europe, companies reward their people with tax-efficient benefits - gym memberships, travel, courses, meals, wellness - funded as company credit rather than taxed salary. It is one of the most powerful levers a company has to attract and keep talent. In Albania the model is almost absent, and where benefit schemes exist anywhere, they tend to be dull: an employee logs in once a quarter, spends a budget out of obligation, and forgets the app exists.

This challenge is to build the marketplace that fixes both problems at once - giving employees benefits they genuinely want, and giving companies an easy, modern way to compete on what they offer their people. Build it so it could work in any country, but make it shine for Albania.
What you are building
A two-sided benefits marketplace with three actors:
›
Employees browse the marketplace and choose the offers, deals, and packages they want.
›
Employers fund and approve those choices.
›
Service providers - gyms, travel agencies, restaurants, clinics, retailers, telecoms - list their offers and deals.
The core loop
An employee browses and selects one or more offers - a single deal or a package that may span several providers. The employer reviews and approves the selection, and payment goes directly to the relevant provider or providers. The employee enjoys the benefit; the money never passes through their hands.

A company can join the platform in either role - as an employer giving its people a welfare budget, or as a provider offering its services and deals to the marketplace (or both). Your app should make onboarding for each role clear and fast.
Make it international, win it in Albania
Architect the product so it is not locked to one country - think multiple languages, multiple currencies, and benefit categories that can be localized per market. Then prove it where it matters: design the primary experience around Albania, with local providers, Albanian Lek, and benefits that make sense here. The strongest solutions feel built for a Tirana employee today, yet could open in Milan or Madrid tomorrow without a rewrite.
Make it a habit, not a chore
Most benefit apps are opened once a quarter. Yours should be one people want to return to. Give them reasons: new deals worth checking, recommendations that feel personal, a sense of discovery, moments of delight. Engagement and stickiness are explicit goals of this challenge - treat "would I open this again next week?" as a question your design has to answer with yes.
Where AI comes in
AI should be part of the product, not an afterthought. Use it where it makes the experience smarter and more human - for example, personalized recommendations, a conversational way to find the right benefit, smart bundling of offers into packages, or insights for employers on what their people value. Using AI tools to accelerate your build is also welcome and encouraged. Show us AI that earns its place.

Scope and ground rules
You build everything from scratch - no datasets, APIs, or infrastructure are provided. Seed your own catalog of sample providers and offers, mock sample employers and employees, and simulate payments (no real payment rail is required). Favor a working core loop over breadth.

Minimum working demo: an employee browses, selects one or more offers (ideally a package spanning more than one provider), and submits the selection; an employer approves it; and the app routes a simulated payment to the relevant provider(s) and confirms the benefit - with at least one AI-driven or engagement feature shown live.
